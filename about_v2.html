About & Manual

Here to Slay DUNGEONS Mapper

This website is a dungeon layout builder for Here to Slay: Dungeons. It lets you place tiles on a hex board, test valid layouts, auto-generate complete dungeons, manage bosses, and save or load setups without needing to wrestle with the physical tiles every time.

This page explains the full normal-user workflow, the important features, and the controls and shortcuts built into the mapper.

Getting Started

When you open the mapper, the board sits in the middle, the tile tray is on the left, and the boss and feedback area is on the right. The entrance tile is placed automatically. The tool then selects six active dungeon tiles and keeps the remaining three in reserve.

Your first job is simple: build a legal dungeon around the entrance tile. You can do that manually by dragging tiles from the tray, or you can let the Auto Build button do the heavy lifting.

  • Choose a tile set from the top bar.
  • Review the six selected tiles in the left drawer.
  • Drag tiles onto the board and rotate them as needed.
  • Watch the feedback panel for placement success or warnings.
  • Add a boss once the dungeon is ready.

Understanding the Layout

Top Bar

The top bar contains the tile set selector, appearance mode control, advanced tools menu, quick actions menu, and the About & Manual link.

Left Drawer

This is the Tile Drawer. It contains the six selected tiles for the current build and the reserve pile for inactive tiles.

Board

The board is the main workspace. It includes the hex grid, entrance tile, reference card, placed dungeon tiles, boss tokens, auto-build button, reset button, and zoom badge.

Right Drawer

This drawer contains the Boss Selection pile and the Placement Feedback area, including checklist progress and status messages.

Tip: If you want more room to work, collapse one or both drawers. The mapper is much easier to read on smaller screens when you keep the side panels under control.

The Board and Core Anchors

The board uses a visible hex-grid system, so tiles snap into position cleanly. The entrance tile is placed first and acts as the anchor point for the dungeon. A reference card sits above it and becomes the snap area for boss placement.

Entrance Tile

The entrance tile is always placed first and cannot be sent back to the tray. Once other tiles connect to it, its rotation becomes much more restricted.

Reference Card

The reference card gives the boss tokens a clear home and keeps the layout organized around the entrance area.

Building Your Dungeon

Placing Tiles

Drag a tile from the tray to the board. As it moves over the play area, the mapper snaps it to the nearest hex position and checks whether the current spot is valid. If the tile meets the placement rules, it stays. If it fails, the feedback panel tells you why.

Rotating Tiles

Hovering over a tile reveals the rotate controls. You can rotate tiles in either direction to line up faces and improve legal contact. Already placed tiles can also be rotated, but they will be re-validated immediately.

Legal Placement

The mapper enforces the dungeon placement rules in normal use. A tile needs enough valid contact with the current layout, overlaps are rejected, and blocked faces on the entrance tile are respected.

What Happens When Something Fails

Invalid placements are clearly marked and explained. Depending on the situation, a bad tile may be pushed toward an open hex or sent back to the tray after a short delay.

Placement Feedback

Classic Feedback

The default mode gives you straightforward green/red placement feedback at the tile level.

Face-by-Face Feedback

You can enable face-based placement feedback in Advanced Tools to see more detailed connection information.

Status Messages

The right drawer explains why a tile is valid, invalid, returning to the tray, or causing some other change in state.

Checklist

The feedback checklist tracks whether all six tiles are placed and whether a boss has been selected.

Auto Build

If you want a complete dungeon quickly, use the Auto Build button on the board. The mapper selects and places the six active tiles in a valid arrangement around the entrance tile, then also places a random boss.

Auto Build is useful when you want a fast starting point, a random layout to play from, or just a break from dragging and rotating everything manually.

If the auto-builder cannot find a legal result, the status panel will tell you and suggest trying again or rerolling the tile selection.

Tile Management

Reroll Tiles

The reroll button in the Selected Tiles section gives you a fresh set of tray tiles while leaving already placed tiles alone.

Reserve Tiles

The reserve pile contains the inactive tiles. Click it to open Reserve Edit Mode, where the reserve tiles fan out so you can swap them with tray tiles.

Swapping

Select a reserve tile, then select a tray tile to swap them. This gives you more control without wiping the whole layout.

Boss Cards

The Boss Selection pile is in the right drawer. You can click the pile to cycle available bosses, use the random-boss dice button, or drag the top boss card onto the board manually.

Once placed, boss tokens snap to magnet positions around the reference card. They can be moved later, and dragging a placed boss back to the pile or info drawer returns it cleanly.

Boss Placement

Click to cycle bosses, drag to place them, or let the random-boss button choose one for you. Once on the board, bosses stay aligned to the reference area instead of floating around like lost side quests.

Saving, Loading, and Resetting

Save Layout

Save the current state as a JSON file so you can reload the layout later.

Load Layout

Load a previously saved JSON layout. The mapper restores the board state and switches tile set if needed.

Reset Tiles

Use Quick Actions to return tiles to the tray without changing the current tile set.

Board Reset

The reset button on the board clears tiles and bosses and puts the whole setup back at its starting state.

Appearance, Themes, and Layout Controls

The appearance mode control lets you switch between Light, Dark, and System mode. The mapper remembers light and dark preferences separately, so changing modes does not scramble your style choices.

The theme selector in Advanced Tools lets you choose a specific UI theme. Each dungeon set has light and dark theme variants, but you can pick another one if you prefer.

Both side drawers can also be collapsed independently. That makes the board much easier to work with when you want maximum layout space.

Theme-aware page styling

This page is set up to use the same core CSS variables as the main app, so the About page can follow the chosen color theme instead of feeling like it belongs to a completely different website.

Keyboard Shortcuts

Key What It Does When It Works
R Rotate the hovered or selected tile clockwise. When a tile is hovered or selected and you are not typing into an input.
F Rotate the hovered or selected tile counter-clockwise. When a tile is hovered or selected and you are not typing into an input.
F Collapse or expand both drawers. Only when no tile is currently hovered or selected.
Esc Close open dropdowns and small menus. For appearance mode, theme menus, quick actions, and similar popups.

Important: The F key does double duty. If a tile is active, it rotates. If no tile is active, it toggles the drawer layout.

Mouse and Pointer Controls

Drag Tiles

Drag tiles from the tray to the board, or drag placed tiles back toward the drawer to return them.

Pan the Board

Click and drag empty board space to move the current view.

Zoom

Use the mouse wheel over the board to zoom in and out. Click the zoom badge to reset the view.

Drag Bosses

Drag the top boss card to the board, or reposition a boss token that is already placed.

Use Reserve Edit Mode

Click the reserve pile to fan it out, then click reserve and tray tiles to swap them.

Hover Controls

Hovering a tile reveals rotation buttons and also makes that tile the shortcut target.

Available Tile Sets

Each tile set has its own dungeon style and matching light/dark theme pair. The exact availability depends on which assets are currently complete in the mapper.

Molten

Volcanic heat, ember glow, scorched stone, and forge-like energy.

Overgrown

Creeping plant life, swampy greens, roots, moss, and living growth.

Deep Freeze

Cold teal, glacial surfaces, frozen air, and sharp icy mood.

Submerged

Aquatic blues, cyan glow, and an energized underwater feel.

Dreamscape

Soft lavender fantasy, moonlit magic, and astral glow.

Nightmare

Corrupted magenta, bruised plum, and a more hostile fantasy look.

Helpful Tips

  • Use Auto Build when you want a fast starting point, then adjust the layout manually.
  • Collapse the drawers if you want more board space for dragging and zooming.
  • Use reserve swapping when the tray is close to what you want but not quite right.
  • Enable face-based placement feedback if you want more detailed connection guidance.
  • Save layouts you really like instead of trusting memory. Memory is confident and wrong far too often.
